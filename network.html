The yellow nodes are the characters that do not have a gender labeled on their fandom wiki, they are also the smallest nodes indicating they are not very connected. There seems to be a lot of well connected characters in our network. This indicates that there are more than one main character in the series. Classification of the network

Now let's look at our network distribution compared to a random network. Right away we see the difference between the random network and our network of The Office. Just from this comparison we can say that the network of The Office is not a random network. To confirm this we will look at the power exponent of degree distributions.
We can use the power exponent to determine what type of regime we are in. To do so we fit a powerlaw distirbution to our degree distribution. The results from the powerlaw fit are given in the table.

Distribution Degree exponent
Degree 2.1488
In-degree 1.7756
Out-degree 2.2873

We see that the total and out degree distributions are greater than 2, we know that this is a Scale-free regime or Ultra Small World because the degree exponent is 2 < γ < 3. This is what you would expect from a social network. These types of networks have the property that the distances between nodes is small because of hubs that reduce the distances significantly. We also saw this in our visualization: we had quite a lot of hubs that connected our smaller nodes. This is also what we say with our main characters being the most connected character - our hubs, reducing the distances between our network significantly. However our in-degree distribution does not fall into the Ultra small world it falls into the Anomalous Regime γ ≤ 2. In this Regime is also where we see scale free network, but here we have that the "number of links connected to the largest hub grows faster than the size of the network". This can only occur when we have a small network as larger networks cannot work if a hub is expecting more links than available nodes. Since our network is very small it makes sense that it could exist here.
